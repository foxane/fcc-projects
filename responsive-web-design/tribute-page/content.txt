1940 (September 4): Abdurrahman Wahid is born in Jombang, East Java, Indonesia, into a prominent Muslim family.

1952: Wahid's father, Wahid Hasyim, passes away. His father was an influential figure in Indonesian politics and Islamic organizations.

1963: Wahid travels to Cairo, Egypt, to study at Al-Azhar University, one of the world's most prestigious centers of Islamic learning.

1971: Wahid co-founds the Nahdlatul Ulama (NU) Student Association, which becomes an influential organization within Indonesia's largest Islamic organization, Nahdlatul Ulama.

1984: He becomes the chairman of Nahdlatul Ulama, a position he holds until 1999.

1989: Wahid co-founds the National Awakening Party (PKB), which represents the moderate Islamic political movement in Indonesia.

1994: He receives the Magsaysay Award for his leadership in promoting democracy and human rights in Indonesia.

1999 (October): Abdurrahman Wahid is elected as the President of Indonesia by the People's Consultative Assembly (MPR) after the fall of President Suharto's regime. He becomes the first democratically elected president of Indonesia.

2001 (July): Wahid is impeached and removed from office by the People's Consultative Assembly due to allegations of corruption and incompetence. He is succeeded by Vice President Megawati Sukarnoputri.

2009 (December 30): Abdurrahman Wahid passes away in Jakarta, Indonesia, at the age of 69.

Throughout his life, Abdurrahman Wahid, also known affectionately as "Gus Dur," played a significant role in Indonesian politics and Islamic affairs. He advocated for democracy, pluralism, and tolerance, and his contributions continue to inspire many Indonesians today.
